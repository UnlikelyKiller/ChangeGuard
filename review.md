**Findings**

- `High` Service-map risk signals do not survive `impact` analysis. [`populate_service_map()`](/C:/dev/ChangeGuard/src/commands/impact.rs:1839) pushes new reasons and escalates risk at [src/commands/impact.rs:1955](/C:/dev/ChangeGuard/src/commands/impact.rs:1955), but [`analyze_risk()`](/C:/dev/ChangeGuard/src/impact/analysis.rs:46) later recomputes `packet.risk_level` and replaces `packet.risk_reasons` outright at [src/impact/analysis.rs:506](/C:/dev/ChangeGuard/src/impact/analysis.rs:506) and [src/impact/analysis.rs:518](/C:/dev/ChangeGuard/src/impact/analysis.rs:518). The final report can therefore lose every “multi-service blast radius” reason added by M7-2.

- `High` Renamed and deleted files are excluded from affected-service detection. The snapshot layer now preserves `old_path` for renames in [src/commands/impact.rs:371](/C:/dev/ChangeGuard/src/commands/impact.rs:371) and [src/commands/impact.rs:394](/C:/dev/ChangeGuard/src/commands/impact.rs:394), but [`populate_service_map()`](/C:/dev/ChangeGuard/src/commands/impact.rs:1839) looks up services only by the current `change.path` at [src/commands/impact.rs:1852](/C:/dev/ChangeGuard/src/commands/impact.rs:1852) through [src/commands/impact.rs:1857](/C:/dev/ChangeGuard/src/commands/impact.rs:1857). A pure rename or deletion will often resolve to no `service_name`, so `affected_services` underreports exactly the history-sensitive cases.

- `High` Cross-service edge computation is both ambiguous and incomplete. [`compute_cross_service_edges()`](/C:/dev/ChangeGuard/src/coverage/services.rs:169) maps service ownership only from handler names and data-model names at [src/coverage/services.rs:175](/C:/dev/ChangeGuard/src/coverage/services.rs:175)-[180](/C:/dev/ChangeGuard/src/coverage/services.rs:180). The call graph it consumes is loaded by bare `symbol_name` in [src/index/project_index.rs:1182](/C:/dev/ChangeGuard/src/index/project_index.rs:1182)-[1193](/C:/dev/ChangeGuard/src/index/project_index.rs:1193) and again in impact at [src/commands/impact.rs:1923](/C:/dev/ChangeGuard/src/commands/impact.rs:1923)-[1932](/C:/dev/ChangeGuard/src/commands/impact.rs:1932), even though the schema already stores `qualified_name` at [src/state/migrations.rs:228](/C:/dev/ChangeGuard/src/state/migrations.rs:228)-[248](/C:/dev/ChangeGuard/src/state/migrations.rs:248). Repeated names like `health`, `index`, or `User` will overwrite each other, and calls between ordinary internal symbols are invisible, so edge counts can be both false-positive and false-negative.

- `High` Root-level service assignment can claim the whole repository. `infer_services()` can emit a service with an empty directory for root routes via [src/coverage/services.rs:104](/C:/dev/ChangeGuard/src/coverage/services.rs:104)-[117](/C:/dev/ChangeGuard/src/coverage/services.rs:117). The persistence step then turns empty or `.` directories into `LIKE '%'` at [src/index/project_index.rs:1323](/C:/dev/ChangeGuard/src/index/project_index.rs:1323)-[1333](/C:/dev/ChangeGuard/src/index/project_index.rs:1333). In any mixed repo, one root handler can stamp unrelated files with the same `service_name`, making later impact results order-dependent and misleading.

- `Medium` The implementation ignores two of its advertised inputs and all new service config controls. [`infer_services()`](/C:/dev/ChangeGuard/src/coverage/services.rs:21) takes `_call_graph` and `_topology` but never uses them, and it returns immediately when `routes` is empty at [src/coverage/services.rs:27](/C:/dev/ChangeGuard/src/coverage/services.rs:27). Meanwhile `coverage.services.enabled` and `cross_service_elevation_threshold` exist at [src/config/model.rs:518](/C:/dev/ChangeGuard/src/config/model.rs:518)-[533](/C:/dev/ChangeGuard/src/config/model.rs:533), but indexing and impact invoke the feature unconditionally at [src/commands/index.rs:112](/C:/dev/ChangeGuard/src/commands/index.rs:112) and [src/commands/impact.rs:211](/C:/dev/ChangeGuard/src/commands/impact.rs:211). So worker-only services remain invisible, and operators cannot disable or tune the behavior.

- `Low` The new service-map payload is not bounded by context truncation. `ImpactPacket` adds `service_map_delta` at [src/impact/packet.rs:453](/C:/dev/ChangeGuard/src/impact/packet.rs:453)-[461](/C:/dev/ChangeGuard/src/impact/packet.rs:461), but [`truncate_for_context()`](/C:/dev/ChangeGuard/src/impact/packet.rs:586) clears many enrichment fields at [src/impact/packet.rs:625](/C:/dev/ChangeGuard/src/impact/packet.rs:625)-[634](/C:/dev/ChangeGuard/src/impact/packet.rs:634) and never trims this one. Large service snapshots can now survive truncation and bloat downstream prompt/context usage.

- Add rename and delete coverage that uses `old_path` and validates `affected_services` for both cases.

- Add config wiring tests for `coverage.services.enabled` and `cross_service_elevation_threshold`; they are currently defined but not enforced.
